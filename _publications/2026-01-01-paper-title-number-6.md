Abstract: We study the forecasting performance of dispersion in firm-specific signals for cross-sectional stock returns. We have three main findings: (i) dispersion exhibits negatively predictive power, and this result remain robust after screening out the idiosyncratic volatility-related component and extends the Miller’s overpricing story in terms of investor optimism; (ii) dispersion behaves as a positive predictor among the extremely small-sized universe, and prospect theory helps explain for the asymmetric divergence—return relation; and (iii) heterogeneity in momentum and trading frictions shows more strength in return predictability than that in the other fundamental categories and can asymmetrically forecast returns across size and value portfolios.
