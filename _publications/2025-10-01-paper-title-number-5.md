Abstract: We apply Natural Language Processing to Wall Street Journal news and construct the maximum topic coverage ratio (MTR), to capture narrative salience in each period. MTR positively predicts stock market returns in both in-sample and out-of-sample tests and delivers sizable economic gains for mean–variance investors. We link MTR to memory theory and show that it may serve as a cue that activates investors’ memory of market risk. Both narrative similarity and salience contribute significantly to this memory-driven predictability. We provide several robust evidence that shifts in risk beliefs rather than investor underreaction, better explain return predictability.
