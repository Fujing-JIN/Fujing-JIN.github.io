Abstract: We study whether sentiment conveyed through videos predicts stock returns. Using a large language model, we construct a daily market-level visual sentiment index (SVS) from facial expressions in short videos posted by influencers on Douyin and compare it with a caption-based textual sentiment measure (STS). SVS predicts future market returns through an initial positive response followed by partial reversal, with the predictability concentrated in intraday rather than overnight returns. We further show that SVS contains predictive information distinct from STS, and textual sentiment dampens the initial positive price response associated with visual sentiment. Additional tests support a sentiment-based interpretation of SVS: its predictive effects are more pronounced in upward markets, retail-oriented portfolios and active trading environments, while disagreement between SVS and STS also predicts trading volume. Overall, short-video visual sentiment contains market-relevant information beyond accompanying texts and helps explain the timing and dynamics of sentiment-driven price responses.
